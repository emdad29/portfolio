Geological FieldExperience

Sitakund Anticline, Chittagong, Bangladesh

This study focuses on the Sitakund Hill Range, characterized by parallel hill ranges trending NNW-SSE. Conducted by first-year students from the Department of Geology and Mining at the University of Barishal, it specifically investigates the area between the Labanakhya Chara and Barabkund sections. Sitakund Hill Range, situated in the northwestern hilly region of Chittagong, features a doubly plunging, box-like, asymmetric anticline, approximately 68km long and 12km wide. The anticline's axis runs parallel to the hill range, with a monoclonal flexure and reverse fault on its eastern and steeper western flanks, respectively. The western flank is notably affected by a major fault, evident in abrupt topographic changes and major irregularities. The area predominantly comprises sedimentary rocks from the upper tertiary (Miocene) period, with fossil evidence lacking for precise boundary determination.

Kaptai Rangamati Road-cut section, Rangamati, Bangladesh

This study focuses on an area characterized by parallel hill ranges trending NNW-SSE, investigated by second-year students from the Department of Geology and Mining, University of Barishal. It examines the Sitapahar Anticline and its adjacent regions, an asymmetrical fold resulting from compressional forces during the Mio-Pliocene. The study encompasses physiography, geomorphology, structure-stratigraphy, petrography, interpretation, correlation with standard geologic succession, and economic geology.

Jaintiaput-Tamabil-Jaflong Section, Sylhet, Bangladesh

This field aims at geologically characterizing the area as an outer reflection of a monocline that trends nearly E-W, which is investigated by the students of 3rd year, Department of Geology and Mining, University of Barishal. It deals with the physiography, geomorphology, structure, stratigraphy, petrography and its interpretation, correlation with standard geologic succession, economic geology of Janitiapur-Tamabil, Sylhet, Northeastern Bangladesh, along with the facies analysis and interpretation of paleo-environment of depositional history.

Environmental Field Camp -2023 at Kuakata Sea Beach, Barishal, Bangladesh

The Environmental Field Camp 2023, held at Kuakata Sea Beach and hosted by the EAGE Student Chapter of the University of Barishal, focused on analyzing beach profiles, examining beach materials, assessing waste on the beach and in the sea, and studying sand deposition columns. This event provided participants with a practical understanding of coastal environmental processes and the impacts of human activities on marine ecosystems. Through detailed hands-on activities, the camp aimed to deepen participants' knowledge of coastal conservation challenges, equipping them with the insights needed to address these issues effectively.
